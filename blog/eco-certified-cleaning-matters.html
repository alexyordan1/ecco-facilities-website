Why eco-certified cleaning products matter for your building

Going green doesn't mean giving up clean. Here's what eco-certified actually means — and why it matters for your employees' health and the environment.

By the Ecco Facilities team  ·  March 8, 2026

When you hear "eco-friendly cleaning products," you might think about price. You might imagine a trade-off: cheaper chemicals that work less well, or expensive green products that clean worse. But that's a false choice. Here's what eco-certified products actually mean, and why they're worth choosing.

What does "eco-certified" actually mean?

Eco-certified isn't a vague marketing term. It's based on rigorous standards from third-party certifiers like Green Seal and EPA Safer Choice. These organizations test products against specific criteria: toxicity levels, biodegradability, environmental impact, and manufacturing practices. A product gets certified only if it meets those benchmarks.

Green Seal certification, for example, requires that products contain no ingredients classified as hazardous to humans or the environment. EPA Safer Choice products must demonstrate reduced human health and ecotoxicological hazards compared to alternatives. These certifications aren't easy to achieve. They require transparency and testing.

The health impact on your employees

Here's what conventional cleaning chemicals can do to the people working in your space: they can trigger asthma attacks, cause allergic reactions, and create respiratory irritation that leads to sick days. Research consistently links harsh cleaning chemicals to more respiratory issues among the people exposed to them day after day.

The issue isn't just acute exposure. It's cumulative. Day after day, your employees breathe in volatile organic compounds, quaternary ammonium compounds, and other toxins. Over time, these create sensitivity. Some employees develop asthma where they didn't have it before. Others find their existing asthma worsening. This isn't a minor side effect. It's a health problem you're creating by choosing the wrong cleaning products.

Eco-certified products help avoid these issues. They clean effectively without the harsh residue and fumes. Your employees breathe cleaner air. That's not just better for people; it's better for your business.

The environmental impact beyond your building

Harsh chemicals don't disappear after they're used. They go down the drain and into the water system. They persist in the environment. They harm aquatic life. They contaminate groundwater. This isn't hypothetical; it's happening at scale.

Eco-certified products are biodegradable. They break down naturally without leaving toxic residue. They don't accumulate in the environment. And they're safer for the people handling them during manufacturing and distribution. When you choose eco-certified products, you're making a choice that extends beyond your building.

The cost question: green is not more expensive

There's a persistent myth that eco-certified products cost more. Sometimes they do. Sometimes they don't. What matters is cost per clean. An eco-certified product that cleans more effectively might actually be cheaper per application than a harsh chemical that requires more volume.

Beyond the direct product cost, consider the hidden costs of harsh chemicals: increased sick days, higher turnover due to employee health issues, potential liability if someone has a serious reaction, and the reputational impact if your building is known for poor air quality. When you factor those in, eco-certified products are an investment that pays for itself.
