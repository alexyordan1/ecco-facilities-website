What a day porter does

The visible standard, all day.

Janitorial happens after hours. A day porter is the polish your team and your visitors see in real time.

Most common

Lobby & reception polish

Glass doors, security desk, elevator landings, lobby seating. Wiped down on a continuous rotation through the day.

Restroom rotation

Hourly checks: paper, soap, towels, sinks, mirrors. The restrooms are never the reason someone notices the cleaning.

Spill & mess response

Coffee, food, an over-watered plant. Cleaned and dried within minutes — no slip risk, no lingering smell.

Conference setup & teardown

Resetting tables, wiping surfaces, removing dishes, prepping the next room. Quiet handoffs between meetings.

Trash & recycling

Continuous pickup. Bins never overflow. Visible cans look intentional, not neglected.

Packages & light maintenance

Light-bulb swap, package routing to recipients, water cooler refill, simple handyman tasks. Inside the scope, never outside it.

Frequently asked

Day porter questions, answered.

What is a day porter?

A day porter is a dedicated cleaning professional on-site during your business hours — typically 8am to 6pm — to keep your space visibly polished. Unlike janitorial crews that work after hours, a day porter handles real-time issues: spills, restroom restocking, lobby polish, conference setup, package handling.

Do I need a day porter or janitorial cleaning?

Janitorial is the deep cleaning that happens after hours. A day porter is the visible polish during business hours. Most Class-A lobbies, gyms, and high-traffic offices need both. We can recommend the right mix during a free 24-hour quote.

How much does a day porter cost in NYC?

Pricing depends on hours per day (4, 6, or 8), days per week, and the type of space. We provide a transparent written proposal within 24 hours of your initial inquiry, with no obligation.

Which neighborhoods do you serve?

All five boroughs of NYC: Manhattan (Midtown, Financial District, Chelsea, SoHo, Hudson Yards), Brooklyn (Williamsburg, DUMBO, Downtown), Queens, Bronx, and Staten Island.
