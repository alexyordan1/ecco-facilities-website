Section 03 · Approach
Section 03 · Approach

How we think about clean.

Three principles that quietly govern everything we do. They are the answer to the question: why would this be different?

01

Dedicated, not dispatched.

Your team is assigned to your account. They know your space, your priorities, your quiet preferences. No rotation of unfamiliar faces; no dispatched crews arriving late from another building across town. When a team member is out, the replacement has been pre-introduced to your space and your account file — they arrive knowing what matters.

02

Measured, not rushed.

We build schedules that allow for the work to be done properly — not squeezed between other accounts. Our crews are assigned to no more than two buildings per shift, with generous travel time in between. A tight schedule is the first cause of missed details; our answer is to not build tight schedules.

03

Transparent, not invisible.

Monthly performance reports, visible digital checklists, a direct line to your account manager that does not route through a call center. You should always know where your service stands. And on the rare occasion something is missed, the correction arrives before you have finished typing the email.

These three principles are not promises — they are the cost structure of our business. We pay more, we staff more, we invest more in the account file than most competitors. The price you see in Section 06 reflects this choice.

Section 07 · Quality Assurance
Section 07 · Quality Assurance

How we keep it right.

Quality is a system, not a promise. These are the four mechanisms that make the promise real — and the consequences when they fail.

Mechanism 01

The daily checklist

Every visit, your crew completes a 42-point checklist on tablet. Weekly digest with timestamps and Lead Technician signature. Never blank — every item marked complete or flagged with note.

Mechanism 02

The monthly walk-through

Your Account Manager walks the space with you once per month. Documented with photos. Concerns resolved before the next walk — or escalated in writing to leadership.

Mechanism 03

The 24-hour guarantee

Something wrong — anything — call, text, or email. A crew returns within 24 hours, no charge, no debate. We do not ask you to prove it; if you noticed, it matters.

Mechanism 04

The satisfaction audit

Quarterly 3-question survey. If our rolling score falls below 4.0/5.0 for two consecutive quarters, we restructure your team at our cost — or part ways without penalty, your choice.

Our Promise You should be able to forget we exist, except for the clean reception every Monday morning. That is what you are buying.

Section 08 · Sustainability
Section 08 · Sustainability

Cleaning, responsibly.

Not a marketing line. A methodology. The word in our name — Ecco — is the one we had to earn.

The cleaning industry has a quiet carbon and chemical footprint: ammonia-based solvents, single-use microfiber, gas-powered equipment, mystery imports labeled "eco" without evidence. We have chosen to build a counter-example. Every choice below is documented and auditable.

GS-37 Certified

100% Green Seal products

Every product we use is Green Seal GS-37 certified. No exceptions. No "green when convenient." We publish our full product list on request.

Zero waste goal

Microfiber over single-use

Color-coded cloths, laundered weekly in our own facility, replaced on a 90-day rotation. No paper beyond restroom necessities.

HEPA · Electric

Battery, not gasoline

Our vacuums and scrubbers are HEPA-filtered and electric. Quieter, cleaner, no CO emissions in your space. Batteries recycled end-of-life.

Audited chain

Traceable supply chain

We source from three domestic suppliers whose practices we have audited. No mystery imports. Full chain of custody on request.

Our clients' sustainability reports include our service data on request. We provide the documentation, audit trails, and certifications that make ESG reporting defensible.

Why partner with Ecco.

A brief case for partnership — for the partner deciding between us and three similar proposals.

01

We are built for accounts, not routes.

Most cleaning companies optimize around routes — fitting as many stops as possible into a shift. We optimize around accounts — fitting as few stops as possible into a crew's shift, so the work can breathe. This is more expensive to run. It is also the reason our work does not feel rushed.

02

Our people stay.

Industry average crew turnover is 210% annually. Ours is under 35%. The same faces, the same standards, the same care, year over year. Turnover is not a labor problem — it is a quality problem dressed up as a labor problem.

03

We write it down.

Every detail — your preferred trash-bag color, which door to use first, the plant in reception that needs no watering — lives in your account file. When a team member is out, the replacement arrives knowing these things. Institutional memory is a practice, not an accident.
